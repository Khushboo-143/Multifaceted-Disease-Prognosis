Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. There are three main types of diabetes: type 1, type 2, and gestational diabetes.

Symptoms:

  • Increased thirst and hunger
  • Frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision

Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect your heart. It includes diseases of the blood vessels, such as coronary artery disease, heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias), and heart defects you're born with (congenital heart defects).

Symptoms:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Dizziness or fainting
Predict Heart Disease

Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement. It develops gradually, sometimes starting with a barely noticeable tremor in just one hand.

Symptoms:

  • Tremors or shaking
  • Stiffness of the limbs and trunk
  • Slowness of movement
  • Impaired balance and coordination
  • Speech changes
Predict Parkinson's Disease

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that starts in the cells of the breast. It can occur in both men and women, but it is far more common in women.

Symptoms:

  • New lump in the breast or underarm
  • Changes in the size, shape, or appearance of the breast
  • Unexplained pain in the breast or nipple
  • Nipple discharge, other than breast milk
  • Skin changes on the breast
Predict Breast Cancer
